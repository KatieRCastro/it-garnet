Park Site reflection and feedback

The park microsite is the second time I have been assigned to create a website on my own. This time around I was able to approach the assignment with more confidence in my knowledge.I felt more comfortable in branching out and exploring different designs. I think I did well when approaching the home page, I am really proud of the inclusion of the park video and the navigational bar. I think I could be better at exploring different layouts with different positioning techniques.The feedback I received helped me add more design features to tie the site together, before, my site pages were pretty simple, so I went in and added more color.I liked the minimalistic feel of the website but it definitely benefitted from the added color.I really enjoyed being able to pick from a wide variety of parks, It gave me creative freedom and made the process fun. After this project, I feel confident enough in my skills to create another website on my own.I understand that this field is forever changing , but I now feel confident in searching for what I need because I have a better understanding of the basics.

Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement describes and emphasizes the importance of designing with a user first mentality.The article emphasizes the importance of establishing a solid foundation that performs well and is usable across all browsers.It is important to focus on the usability and experience rather than new interactivity and technology that does not perform well on older browsers.That being said, older browsers should always be considered when designing, excluding them is discriminatory.
